Selected work

Projects and roles that became useful.

Infrastructure

Home systems lab

What started as a Minecraft server became a two-machine stack with Docker services for media, network-wide ad blocking, local AI tools, home automation, tunnels, monitoring, and self-hosted storage.

  • Designed around automatic recovery after outages and accidental shutdowns.
  • Learned Linux, networking, reverse proxies, mounts, and orchestration by solving real failures.
  • Shifted from technical sandbox to household utility used daily by family.

Startup

Co-founded a small product studio and built an AI voicemail agent that answered, responded to, transcribed, and summarized calls for business owners.

The product reached real trials, then wound down when the demo needed more work before it could become something customers would pay for.

About

I learn technologies because my projects demand them.

My work usually starts with a practical problem: a service that breaks after an outage, a team that needs someone to organize it, or a product idea that has to survive a real customer conversation. I like the point where curiosity turns into responsibility.

I am aiming toward electrical and computer engineering because I want to understand the hardware beneath the software I already build. The long-term pull is embedded systems, infrastructure, and computing that has to keep working under pressure.
